Volunteers

A volunteer is someone who is looking to lend their time to provide non-clinical care for vulnerable people within their local health and social care services.

Those working within the below professions are particularly encouraged to join, due to having an active DBS record:

  • St John Ambulance or Red Cross Volunteer
  • Police
  • Teacher
  • Dental worker
  • Veterinary worker

If you work as any of these, you can sign up to the National Care Force as a healthcare worker.

How do I get involved?

Once you've signed up to the National Care Force and have passed compliance screening, you'll be able apply for shifts available on the platform.

This will mean covering non-clinical staffing gaps in a variety of social care settings. Examples of non-clinical care include fetching shopping and essentials for people in self-isolation or providing in-home care such as cleaning and laundry.

Such work will support care professionals and ease the strain to services caused by extra users and staffing gaps during coronavirus.
